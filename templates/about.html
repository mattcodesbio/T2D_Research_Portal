Exploring Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs) among South Asian Populations

Project Overview

This web application analyzes genetic variants associated with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D) prevalence in South Asian populations. Our platform integrates genomic data with clinical insights to identify potential genetic risk factors. Users can explore these genetic variants, their frequencies and significance across South Asian (SAS) subpopulations.


Scientific Background

Type 2 diabetes (T2D) affects over 463 million people globally, with South Asians facing higher prevalence rates. While environmental factors are important, genetic susceptibility also contributes. Recent studies show that certain genetic variants are more prevalent in South Asian populations, suggesting a greater risk of developing the disease.
