Historical Population Dynamics of South Asian Populations

The graph illustrates changes in the effective population size of South Asian populations (BEB, STU, PJL, ITU, GIH) over generations, on a logarithmic scale. Historical population bottlenecks, leads to stronger genetic drift with declines in genetic diversity in small population. However, as the population sizes recovered and expanded, the positive selection has had played a dominant role, retaining the beneficial traits related to environmental adaptations, glucose metabolism and energy storage. This might have historically supported the survival of south asian populations in environments with fluctuating availability of food.
